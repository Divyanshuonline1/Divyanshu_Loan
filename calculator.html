Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about loans and calculations

How is the monthly payment calculated?

The monthly payment is calculated using the standard amortization formula:

PMT = P × [r(1+r)^n] ÷ [(1+r)^n - 1]

Where:

  • PMT = Monthly payment
  • P = Principal (loan amount)
  • r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate divided by 12)
  • n = Total number of payments (loan term in years × 12)

Why does the interest rate make such a big difference?

Even a small change in interest rate can significantly impact your monthly payment and the total amount you'll pay over the life of the loan. This is because interest compounds over time, and the effect is magnified with longer loan terms.

For example, on a ₹25,00,000 home loan with a 20-year term, a 1% difference in interest rate could mean paying several lakhs more in total interest.

Should I choose a shorter or longer loan term?

This depends on your financial situation and goals:

  • Shorter term: Higher monthly payments but less total interest paid and faster debt freedom
  • Longer term: Lower monthly payments but more total interest paid over the life of the loan

Use our calculator to compare different scenarios and find the right balance for your budget.

How accurate is this calculator?

Our calculator provides a good estimate of your loan payments based on the information you provide. However, actual loan terms may vary based on:

  • Your credit score and history
  • Lender-specific policies and fees
  • Loan processing fees and other charges
  • Prepayment options and penalties

For a precise calculation specific to your situation, we recommend speaking with one of our loan advisors.
